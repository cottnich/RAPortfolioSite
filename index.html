Introduction

Hi! My name is Nicholas Cotton, and I’m so excited to be your RA this year. Outside of my studies in Math and Computer Science, I love exploring vintage tech, tinkering with projects (like modding my espresso machine), and making music on my trombone and bass. I also develop tools to enhance my Linux environment—it's a fun way to combine creativity and problem-solving.

On a personal note, I’m half Okinawan, and I hold a deep connection to my Pacific Island and Japanese heritage. Sharing stories and connecting over diverse backgrounds is something I value, and I hope to foster a community where we all feel comfortable being ourselves.

What is an RA?

Being an RA means building a supportive and welcoming community where residents can thrive. It’s about being a mentor, a listener, and a problem-solver. Whether it’s a late-night chat about life or organizing a fun event, I’m here to help you make the most of your time at Penn State.

Impact of Community Builders

Community builders are at the heart of what makes residence life meaningful. My goal is to create spaces where everyone feels included and supported. One of my favorite events this semester was hosting a Super Smash Bros. Melee Tournament in collaboration with PSU’s Smash Club. While attendance wasn’t massive, it brought together a passionate group of people for an unforgettable night of competition and camaraderie. I hope residents gain new friendships, confidence, and a sense of belonging from the events we host.

Transferable Skills

Here are some skills I’ve developed as an RA that I’ll take into my future career:

  • Conflict Resolution: Handling delicate situations has taught me to navigate complex interpersonal dynamics, a skill essential for teamwork in any field.
  • Organization: Managing events and adminatrative tasks as an RA sharpens my ability to prioritize and stay organized, even under pressure.
  • Empathy: Supporting residents with compassion and understanding makes me a better collaborator, especially in roles that require user-focused solutions.
  • Communication: From writing professional incident reports to leading discussions, clear and effective communication is a skill I’ll carry forward.
  • Adaptability: Living and working in a dynamic environment has prepared me to tackle unexpected challenges with confidence and flexibility.
